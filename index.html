Introduction

In May of this year, I had the opportunity to study abroad with the mass media department here at Northwest. 15 students and two facutly members spent a total of 18 days touring around Eurpoe, seeing many of the most famous landmarks in the world along the way. From spending my birthday in Greece to watching a Shakespeare play in London, this trip was packed with unforgettable memories.

The Itinerary

8 countries in 18 days is no small task. Here's a summary of how it all worked:

  • Day 1: New York City, USA

    Times Square, NBC Studios

  • Day 2-4: Athens, Greece

    International Journalism Conference, Acropolis, Temple of Poseidon, explore the city of Athens.

  • Day 5-6: Rome, Italy

    The Colosseum, Forum, Pantheon, Villa Borghese, eat far too much pasta

  • Day 7: Vatican, Italy

    Art Collections, Papal Apartments, Sistine Chapel, St. Peter's Square

  • Day 8: Venice, Italy

    Fish Market, Murano Glass, gondola rides, a lot more pasta and gelato

  • Day 9-10: Budapest, Hungary

    Parliament, Buda Castle, National Opera, Szechenyi Baths

  • Day 11-12: Vienna, Austria

    Beethoven's apartment, St. Stephen's Cathedral, Schonbrunn Palace

  • Day 13-14: Munich, Germany

    Dachau, Franken Church, Engligh Garden, Biergarten

  • Day 15-17: London, England

    British Museum, Big Ben, Tower Bridge, Shakespeare's Globe Theatre

  • Day 18: Paris, France

    The Eiffel Tower, a failed attempt to get into the Louvre, Notre Dame Cathedral
